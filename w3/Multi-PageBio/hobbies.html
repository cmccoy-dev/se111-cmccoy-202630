In total, I have counted over 20 active hobbies. So its hard to narrow it down to just 3. But if I had to choose which ones have taken over my life at times and what hobbies are the most persistant in my life, these three would be it.

  1. Fishing

    2. My first memory of me fishing would be when I was about six or seven. Fast Forward a couple years and I'm completely back in it. At least 14 hours a day minimum had to be commited to anything fishing related; Whether that was shopping for new gear, watching youtube, activily fishing, or just talking about. As of right now, this has taken over my life.

  2. Photography / Videography

    3. This is one of my newer hobbies. I got my first camera mid december since the world likes to drag black friday and cyber monday out. However I really wouldn't say it was a hobby till early spring of 2025. At first it was just taking pictures of my cats, then once I started binge watching youtube, it instantly shifted to taking pictures of cars, sports, nature, anything micro, and anytime I travel. As 2025 started to fade I then started to pick up videography as that is a skill I really want to have for all my trips that I go on.

  3. Speedcubing

    4. On the other side of the coin this is probably one of my oldest hobbies. I started around 11 and its been off and on ever since. I'm not sure while solving a cube is so satisfing or fulfilling but every three months of me dropping it, im back at it for four months. And I'm not sure why I always end up dropping it, I think it's just that its such a time eater. If you want to be top 20% even, you have to put in a ridiculous amount of time learning new algs, practicing, and commiting hundreds to memory. I think from now on this is going to be one of my hobbies I do for fun more than for competition.
